August Wilson's House

-built in 1840, Daisy Wilson, August Wilson’s mother, moved in at 1940.

-bought by Paul Ellis Jr in 2005 for $25,000 with the aim to renovate the house as a monument to the rich heritage of the Hill

-the house is now technically called the Daisy Wilson Community Center in honor of his mom

-Denzel Washington helped fund it, and conducted the ground-blessing…didn’t want the spotlight on himself

-Many other celebs, Tyler Perry, Oprah, Samuel and LaTanya Jackson, Laurence Fishburne, Shonda Rhimes, Antoine Fuqua, John McClain, also chipped in

-Also, funding from Richard King Mellon and Hillman Foundations

-Denise Turner is the current acting Chief Executive

August Wilson House before


-2 rooms, 1 of them shared by August Wilson and his 5 siblings. They were able to add 2 more rooms once the family above them moved out
-When August Wilson last visited his house in 1999, it was in decay, with no one living in it anymore
-Paul Ellis Jr, August Wilson’s nephew, bought the house in 2005
-declared a historic landmark in 2008

August Wilson House now
-August Wilson’s original room kept
-Many private benefactors, led by Denzel Washington, put up $7 million to renovate the House. Currently open to the public
-Budget of originally $4-5 million to renovate, exacerbated by the pandemic and supply chain issues to higher cost and delayed renovation
-Hosts various arts programs, in line with the core mission to use August’s legacy to inspire artists
-Backyard has a stage, which puts on a play by August Wilson and a block party every year
-Reopened on August 13th, 2022
